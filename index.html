Field Methods

  • VES

    Vertical Electrical Sounding (VES) is an active geophysical method that measures the electrical properties of rocks by injecting currents into the earth. This method is also developed from the resistivity method.

    This method usually uses the Schlumberger configuration to determine the resistivity variation of rock layers in the vertical direction. The VES method is very useful in the exploration of aquifer aquifers in an area.

  • Microsesimic

    Microseismic method can record microseismic activity to explain its local site effect which is associated with ground vulnerability properties and its response against the seismic wave propagations.

    This data can then be used to map the vulnerable area to arrange appropriate hazard mitigation plans.

  • MASW

    Multi-Channel Analysis of Surface Waves (MASW) is geophysical methods that use surface waves to analyze subsurface conditions. The MASW method can calculate the shear wave velocity (Vs) based on the rayleigh surface wave velocity.

    Rayleigh surface waves can be easily seen using seismic equipment with vertical geophones, this is due to the nature of the ground roll rather than Rayleigh waves.

  • ERT

    Electrical resistivity tomography (ERT) data are collected along a line as a combined soundingâ€“profiling survey, using a multi- electrode resistivity measurement system. This data set can be inverted for the true subsurface resistivity by using 2D or 3D inversion algorithms and the resulting estimated models can be interpreted accordingly.

    Electrical resistivity tomography is a method adapted to the recognition and study of 2D structures perpendicular to the profile and to the identification of the various geological layers, including the surface cover, but is more expensive and time consuming than the electromagnetic survey.

  • Seismic Refraction

    Seismic Refraction method is one of the geophysical methods which is based on measuring the response of seismic waves induced into the earth (surface) and then refracted below the earth's surface.

    By analyzing seismic wave velocities in each lithology, a subsurface model can be made based on the time of wave propagation.
