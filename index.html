It is a great pleasure to introduce the Geophysics Field Camp 2020 Project. Geophysics Field Camp is an annual project organized by final year students of Geophysics Study Program Universitas Gadjah Mada. The project will be managed and carried out independently by the students, so they will learn how to manage research activities, which includes preparing a research proposal, fieldwork, data processing and interpretation, and wrapping up their research into scientific publications.

The topic and location of field camp project vary every year. This year, the students will apply near-surface geophysical methods for urban development in Yogyakarta area. Yes, the 2021’s field camp will take place at Universitas Gadjah Mada campus. This location has been chosen as a response to the pandemic situation that restricts mobility for students conducting fieldwork. In addition to that, the topic and location also introduce the students into the application of geophysical methods beyond natural resources exploration and how geoscience can contribute to city and community resilience.

Following the pandemic protocols, this year field camp will combine offline and online activities. Field measurement (offline) will be conducted at university construction sites to investigate the soil characteristics and around Toya GAMA water facility to investigate the pollution potential. Field measurement will apply strict pandemic protocol provided by the university. After that, students will collaborate to perform data processing and interpretation using the remote working platform. More detail explanation is provided in the proposal.

Field camp project is a good opportunity for the students to make a connection between theory, teaching, and field activities. The knowledge, skills, and experience the students gained during this project are beyond what they can get from classroom teaching and will enrich them as future geoscientists. Therefore, we are very pleased to invite you as a sponsor and to contribute to the successful 2021 Geophysics Field Camp.

Jordy Reformeras.

Field Methods

  • Microsesimic

    Microseismic method can record microseismic activity to explain its local site effect which is associated with ground vulnerability properties and its response against the seismic wave propagations.

    This data can then be used to map the vulnerable area to arrange appropriate hazard mitigation plans.

  • MASW

    Multi-Channel Analysis of Surface Waves (MASW) is geophysical methods that use surface waves to analyze subsurface conditions. The MASW method can calculate the shear wave velocity (Vs) based on the rayleigh surface wave velocity.

    Rayleigh surface waves can be easily seen using seismic equipment with vertical geophones, this is due to the nature of the ground roll rather than Rayleigh waves.

  • ERT

    Electrical resistivity tomography (ERT) data are collected along a line as a combined sounding–profiling survey, using a multi- electrode resistivity measurement system. This data set can be inverted for the true subsurface resistivity by using 2D or 3D inversion algorithms and the resulting estimated models can be interpreted accordingly.

    Electrical resistivity tomography is a method adapted to the recognition and study of 2D structures perpendicular to the profile and to the identification of the various geological layers, including the surface cover, but is more expensive and time consuming than the electromagnetic survey.

  • Seismic Refraction

    Seismic Refraction method is one of the geophysical methods which is based on measuring the response of seismic waves induced into the earth (surface) and then refracted below the earth's surface.

    By analyzing seismic wave velocities in each lithology, a subsurface model can be made based on the time of wave propagation.
